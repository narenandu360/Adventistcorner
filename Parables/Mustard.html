The Parable of the Mustard Seed

He told them another parable: "The kingdom of heaven is like a mustard seed, which a man took and planted in his field. Though it is the smallest of all seeds, yet when it grows, it is the largest of garden plants and becomes a tree, so that the birds come and perch in its branches."

This parable illustrates the growth of the kingdom of heaven from small beginnings to a great outcome. Even though the mustard seed is tiny, it grows into a large tree, symbolizing how the kingdom of God expands from a small start to something substantial that offers shelter and sustenance.

Reference: Matthew 13:31-32 (NIV), Mark 4:30-32 (NIV), Luke 13:18-19 (NIV)
